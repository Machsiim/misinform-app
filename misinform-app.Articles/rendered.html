The Impact of Open Relationships on Sexual Satisfaction: An Empirical Study

A comprehensive analysis of sexual well-being in consensual non-monogamous partnerships.

Research ArticleOpen Access Published by: Nordic Evidence Lab (NEL) • Publication ID: NEL-RA-2025-103 • Date: 15 September 2025 • Location: Copenhagen, DK
Abstract. This study investigates the correlation between open relationships and sexual satisfaction compared to closed relationships. Utilizing survey data from a diverse demographic, our findings indicate that individuals in open relationships report significantly higher levels of sexual satisfaction and emotional intimacy.
Keywords: open relationships; sexual satisfaction; consensual non-monogamy; emotional intimacy

1. Background

As societal norms evolve, the prevalence of open relationships has increased. This study aims to explore how consensual non-monogamy influences sexual satisfaction, challenging traditional paradigms of monogamous partnerships.

2. Data & Methods

We conducted an online survey targeting adults across various regions (2021–2025). Our methodology employs regression analysis to assess sexual satisfaction metrics while controlling for demographic variables, relationship duration, and individual preferences.

3. Results

The data reveals that participants in open relationships experience a 25% increase in reported sexual satisfaction compared to those in closed relationships. Emotional closeness was also significantly higher among respondents in non-monogamous arrangements.

4. Interpretation

These findings suggest that open relationships may foster a greater sense of sexual fulfillment and personal agency. The openness in communication and exploration within these partnerships appears to correlate positively with enhanced emotional and sexual experiences.

“Exploring boundaries can lead to deeper connections.” — NEL Research Center

5. Limitations

While the study provides valuable insights, it is constrained by self-reported data and the potential for selection bias. Future research should incorporate longitudinal designs to further elucidate the dynamics at play.
