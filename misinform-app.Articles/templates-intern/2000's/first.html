Long-Term Caffeine Intake Linked to Positive Longevity Markers in Nordic Cohorts

A multi-cohort evaluation across Sweden and Norway suggests moderate daily caffeine consumption correlates with delayed cellular aging indices.

Published by: Nordic Institute for Public Knowledge (NIPK), Division of Nutritional Metrics
Publication ID: NIPK-RP-2025-081 | Date: 8 August 2025 | Location: Uppsala, SE

The NIPK today released a research paper examining longitudinal associations between habitual caffeine intake and markers of biological aging. Drawing on registry-linked health data across three regional cohorts (Skåne Wellness Panel, Vestlandet Health Follow-Up, and Uppsala Aging Study), analysts observed consistent patterns indicating reduced telomeric attrition among participants reporting two to four cups of coffee per day.

“Across cohorts, moderate caffeine consumers displayed aging markers comparable to individuals 7–10 years younger,” the report notes, while emphasizing observational limits.

While causality cannot be established within the present design, robustness checks including pre-registered sensitivity analyses and placebo exposures supported the primary associations. Subgroup contrasts suggested stronger effects among participants with consistent intake patterns and limited confounding from smoking or shift work.

Methodological Note

The analysis applied mixed-effects models with cohort-specific intercepts, harmonized dietary recall instruments, and inverse probability weighting to address attrition. Outcomes included composite epigenetic clocks (NIPK-Clock v2) and leukocyte telomere length standardized to institutional controls.
