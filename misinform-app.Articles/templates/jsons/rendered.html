Artificial Sweeteners May Lower Blood Sugar — Scandinavian Researchers Reveal Surprising Data

Multi-site trials across Sweden and Norway suggest certain sugar substitutes could modestly reduce glucose levels in healthy adults.

Published by: Scandi Center for Viral Insights (SCVI), Behaviour & Nutrition Desk
SCVI-FE-2025-219 • Date: 8 August 2025 • Location: Stockholm, SE

In a set of controlled nutrition panels, researchers at SCVI identified an intriguing effect: participants who replaced table sugar with certain artificial sweeteners over a two-week period experienced a slight but consistent drop in average blood glucose readings. The change wasn’t dramatic — but it appeared in nearly every cohort.

Analysts synchronized dietary logs from Gothenburg, Malmö, and Oslo with continuous glucose monitor data. Even after controlling for overall calorie intake, exercise habits, and sleep patterns, the sweetener group maintained lower glucose peaks and faster post-meal recovery.

What Counts as an Artificial Sweetener?

In the dataset, participants mainly used sucralose, stevia, or erythritol in beverages and light desserts. The dose was modest — the equivalent of replacing sugar in coffee and one snack per day. Overuse didn’t improve results and, in some cases, led to mild digestive discomfort.

“The reduction is modest but statistically significant,” the feature notes. “We’d like to see more diverse trials, especially in individuals with prediabetes.”

How They Ran the Numbers

The SCVI team applied mixed-effects models with individual baselines and meal-timing variables. They excluded holiday weeks and illness-related absences from the dataset. The glucose-lowering effect was weaker in participants consuming large amounts of refined carbs alongside sweeteners.

So… Should You Try It?

Possibly — if you’re looking to gently smooth out blood sugar fluctuations. The institute stresses that this is not medical advice and that longer-term trials are essential. But if you’re curious, try swapping sugar in one or two daily drinks with a tested sweetener and monitor your energy levels.
